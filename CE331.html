CE 331: Civil Engineering Materials II

Class Background

This class is the second materials class in civil engineering in the Lyle's School of Civil Engineering at Purdue University. The class usually consisted of sophomores and juniors in civil engineering and is a required class for graduation from the Lyles School. The class was broken in two main phases: property behavior relationships of composite materials and specific topics to the materials used in civil engineering applications.

Property behavior relationships included topics such as mechanical properties of continuous and particulate composites, fracture mechanics, viscoelasticity and viscosity. Laboratory sessions during this phase consists mechanical property testing of composite materials, non-destructive testing, measurement of creep in polymers, and a numerical techniques such as least squares to determine viscoelastic parameters.

The class then transitions, and covers materials specific to civil engineers. This primarily consists of aggregates, cementitious materials, and asphalt materials. A small portion of the class at the end of the semester is spent discussing clay and wood. Laboratory topics during this phase of the course is concrete production and fresh testing, concrete mixture proportioning, hardened concrete testing including resistivity testing. Asphalt laboratory sessions cover asphalt production and asphalt mechanical testing.

I was a Graduate Teaching Assistant for this class from Fall 2012 to the Spring of 2015, while it was under the supervision of Professor Weiss. During this time I helped to update the laboratory sessions, including new experiments and updated lab preparation slides. I helped to incorporate areas of research into laboratory topics. I was also responsible for the development of evaluation rubrics and ultimately to evaluate student performance in accordance with those rubrics.
